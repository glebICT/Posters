The Binding of Isaac is a cult indie roguelike by Edmund McMillen, famous for its dark style, high difficulty, and endless replayability.

Gameplay Basics:

Roguelike: Levels are randomly generated. If you die, you lose everything and start over.
Combat: You control Isaac and attack enemies using his tears as projectiles.

Items & Synergies: Hundreds of artifacts modify stats and mechanics. The core feature is "synergies," where item effects combine into powerful combos.

Progression: Defeating bosses unlocks over 30 playable characters and secret floors.
Story: The plot is a dark, modern reimagining of the Biblical story of the same name.

Modern Versions: The current standard is the Rebirth remake (2014) and its expansions: Afterbirth, Afterbirth+, and the final massive DLC, Repentance.
